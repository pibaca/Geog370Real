In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

Mainly, I operated within the instructions that you gave us. I began by opening the world projection map, and then made sure I changed the color to something other than the default. After that, I ran the Indicatrix Mapper to create Caps and Lines within the map. These represent area and angle distortions that are common with each projection. After that, I click on the projections in the bottom right of the page. After that, I changed the coordinate reference systems to the ones the homework requested. The caps and lines adjusted per the projection. I then created a new print layout and label for each projection in order to convert them to PNG files.

Pseudo Mercator Projection

This projection is a good example of sacrificing size in order to preserve angles. This projection conserves angles and shapes, as is shown by the straight lines, but it enlarges sizes at the north and south poles.

Winkel Sphere Projection

The Winkel Sphere projection attempts to find a working balance between angle preservation, shape preservation, and size preservation. As is shown by the oval caps layer, the projection enlarges the poles slightly, but not as much as the mercator projection. From this projection, it is easier to see the curveature of the earth and how different continents fall on a globe perspective.

Cylindrical Sphere Projection

The cylindrical sphere preserves size, shape and angle at the equator line. Everywhere else, size and shape of continents are compromised, both at the north and south poles. This is especially prominent with the extremely stretched ovals at the very top of the northern continents and bottom of the southern continents.

Conic Equidistant Sphere Projection

This is one of the first maps where the bottom of South America and Africa is visibly larger due to distortions. The northern continents appear smaller in size, as they fall in the middle of the map. Antartica is the most distorted feature, as the size and shape is signficiantly different. However, the angles are preserved.

Azimuthal Equidistant Sphere Projection

This map is very similar to the Conic Equidistant map, with the exception that Antartica wraps around the entire sphere, as opposed to getting cut off.

Caster Parabolic Sphere Projection

We see the preservation of angles along the prime meridian and equator, but fairly obvious size and shape distortions at the northern and southern poles.

Equidistant Cylindrical Projection

This projection is similar to the Mercator, in the sense that it distorts the size of the northern poles, but conserves the shape. Near the equator countries are stretched, which distorts the shape and the size. This is done so that the map can conserve all angles.
